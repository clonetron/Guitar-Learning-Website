Meet the Strings

Guitars usually have 6 strings, and if you face one head-on, you'll see that the top string is the thickest, and as you go down, the strings get thinner. Each string has their own name.

In standard guitar tuning, the six strings are usually known as E A D G B E strings respectively, and as seen in the picture. In other tunings, they may be refered to as something different.

Meet the frets

The frets are the those widely-spaced metallic rods on the board of the guitar. However, to avoid confusion, we usually refer to the spaces between the frets as the "frets". This is because when you play guitar, you're putting your finger on those spaces, not on the actual fret.
